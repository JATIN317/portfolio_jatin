🔍 The Problem

Medical billing errors are rampant — most patients are overcharged and never know it. Insurance denials are often wrong but go uncontested because the appeals process is opaque and exhausting. Both problems cost patients thousands of dollars they're legally entitled to keep.

⚡ The Dual-Core Solution

Verity runs two parallel engines: a Bill Analyzer that detects duplicate charges, upcoding, and unbundled services — and an Appeal Builder that identifies wrongful denials and constructs evidence-backed appeal letters. One tool, two fights.

Findings

  • Orange Juice Multipack x6 grew +218%. Standard Cornflakes up +16%. Almond Cornflakes fell -8%.
  • Mango Juice single-serve lost -30% visibility during the campaign rush.

Insight

  • Volume surge was real — but it cannibalized high-margin premium SKUs. Net growth masked margin erosion.

Recommendations

  • Increase staple inventory depth by 30% for future campaigns.
  • Bundle Standard Cornflakes + Milk to drive basket value.
  • Create 6-pack bundles for Coke, Pepsi to capitalize on multipack momentum.
  • Place Mango Juice near billing counter with colorful packaging to recapture visibility.

Findings

  • Kolkata: 51% of spend on Cereals — highest category dependency.
  • Mumbai: only 33% on Cereals, favors Dairy — fast-paced, convenience-driven.
  • Maharashtra inversion: Mumbai vs. Pune behave like opposite markets despite same state.

Insight

  • Kolkata + Kanpur = "Stock-Up" markets. Price-sensitive, family-oriented, weekly haul shoppers.
  • Mumbai = "Milk Run" market. Quick convenience trips, low basket value.

Recommendations

  • Kolkata: Remove slow-moving Drinks from prime shelf space; replace with Bulk Cereal Packs.
  • Kanpur: Launch "Breakfast Bundle" (Cereal + Milk) to capture 80% of wallet share.
  • Pune: "Quick Breakfast Kit" (Cereal + Milk + Instant Coffee) targeting their basket composition.

Findings

  • Kanpur ARPU: ₹3,974 — 53% above average. Highest efficiency.
  • Mumbai: largest user base (10 users), lowest revenue per user (₹1,910).
  • Bangalore: high ARPU and high sales volume — premium customer base.

Insight

  • Mumbai is a "Hollow Growth" trap — high acquisition, low yield. Budget is being wasted acquiring low-value users.

Recommendations

  • Mumbai: Freeze user acquisition. Shift budget to retention and upsell programs.
  • Kanpur: Aggressively scale ad spend to find audiences similar to existing high spenders.

Findings

  • Elite VIP (e.g. 712345388): Fewer items (15), highest revenue. Price-insensitive — buys premium without needing volume.
  • Bulk Whale (e.g. 712345399): Most transactions (20), lower AIV. Frequency-driven, volume-sensitive.

Recommendations

  • Elite VIPs: Market new product launches and premium exclusives. They want novelty, not discounts.
  • Bulk Whales: Market bundles and BOGO offers. They respond to volume incentives.

Insight

  • Top 10 customers are the "Pulse" of the business — a drop in their spend is a leading churn indicator before it hits the wider market.

Recommendations

  • Watchlist Protocol: Track Top 10 accounts weekly. Any cart value drop triggers immediate outreach.
  • Retention Trigger: Cart value drop → personal feedback call.
  • Acquisition Trigger: New entrant breaks into Top list → "Welcome to VIP" promotional vouchers immediately to lock in loyalty.

Findings

  • Traffic peaks weekdays (Tuesday: 129 transactions). Revenue peaks weekends.
  • Sunday shoppers buy 2.5× more per visit despite visiting less frequently.

Insight

  • Volume-Value Paradox: Peak footfall ≠ peak revenue. Weekdays are convenience trips (Milk, Bread). Weekends are pantry-loading missions.

Recommendations

  • Weekday staffing: highest on Mon/Tue for throughput. Open Express Lanes (10 items or less).
  • Weekend staffing: fewer checkout lanes, clear aisles for full-trolley shoppers.
